AC 43.13-1B  Chapter 9: AIRCRAFT SYSTEMS AND COMPONENTS  Section 1: INSPECTION AND MAINTENANCE OF LANDING GEAR

9-6. EMERGENCY SYSTEMS. Exercise
emergency landing gear systems periodically
to ensure proper operation and to prevent inactivity, dirt, and corrosion from rendering the
system inoperative when needed. Most emergency systems employ either mechanical, pressure-bottle, or free-fall extension capabilities.
Check for the proper safeties on triggering
mechanisms, and for the presence of required
placards, and necessary accessories such as
cranks, levers, handles, etc. Emergency blowdown bottles should be checked for corrosion
damage, and then weighed to see if the bottle
is still retaining the charge.
