AC 43.13-1B  Chapter 6: CORROSION, INSPECTION & PROTECTION  Section 7: BASIC CORROSION REMOVAL TECHNIQUES

6-116. CORROSION REMOVAL BY
BLASTING. Abrasive blasting is a process
for cleaning or finishing ferrous metals by directing a stream of abrasive particles against
the surface of the parts. Abrasive blasting is
used for the removal of rust and corrosion and
for cleaning prior to painting or plating. The
following standard blast-cleaning practices
should be adopted.

a. The part to be blast-cleaned should be
removed from the aircraft, if possible. Otherwise, areas adjacent to the part should be
masked or protected from abrasive impingement and system (hydraulic, oil, fuel, etc.)
contamination.
b. Parts should be dry and clean of oil,
grease, or dirt, prior to blast cleaning.
c. Close-tolerance surfaces, such as
bushings and bearing shafts, should be
masked.
d. Blast-clean only enough to remove corrosion coating. Proceed immediately with surface treatments as required.
