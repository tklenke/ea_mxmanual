AC 43.13-1B  Chapter 11: AIRCRAFT ELECTRICAL SYSTEMS  Section 1: INSPECTION AND CARE OF ELECTRICAL SYSTEMS

11-5. BATTERY ELECTROLYTE CORROSION. Corrosion found on or near leadacid batteries can be removed mechanically
with a stiff bristle brush and then chemically
neutralized with a 10 percent sodium bicarbonate and water solution. For Nickel Cadmium (NiCad) batteries, a 3 percent solution
of acetic acid can be used to neutralize the
electrolyte. After neutralizing, the battery
should be washed with clean water and thoroughly dried.
