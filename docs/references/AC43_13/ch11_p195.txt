AC 43.13-1B  Chapter 11: AIRCRAFT ELECTRICAL SYSTEMS  Section 15: GROUNDING AND BONDING

11-195. STATIC-DISCHARGE DEVICE.
Means should be provided to bleed accumulated static charges from aircraft prior to
ground personnel coming in contact with an
aircraft after landing. Normally, there is adequate conductivity in the tires for this, but if
not, a static ground should be applied before
personnel come into contact with the aircraft.
Fuel nozzle grounding receptacles should be
installed in accordance with the manufacturer’s specifications. Grounding receptacles
should provide a means to eliminate the staticinduced voltage that might otherwise cause a
spark between a fuel nozzle and fuel tank access covers and inlets. In addition, static discharging wicks are installed on wings and tail
surfaces to discharge static changes while in
flight.
