AC 43.13-1B  Chapter 11: AIRCRAFT ELECTRICAL SYSTEMS  Section 2: STORAGE BATTERIES

11-16. BATTERY CHARGING. Operation
of storage batteries beyond their ambient temperature or charging voltage limits can result in
excessive cell temperatures leading to electrolyte boiling, rapid deterioration of the cells,
and battery failure. The relationship between
maximum charging voltage and the number of
cells in the battery is also significant. This will
determine (for a given ambient temperature
and state of charge) the rate at which energy is
absorbed as heat within the battery. For leadacid batteries, the voltage per cell must not exceed 2.35 volts. In the case of NiCad batteries,
the charging voltage limit varies with design
and construction. Values of
1.4 and 1.5 volts per cell are generally used. In
all cases, follow the recommendations of the
battery manufacturer.
