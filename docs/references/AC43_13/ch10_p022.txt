10-22. INSTALLATION OF BALLAST.
Ballast is sometimes permanently installed for
c.g. balance purposes as a result of installation
or removal of equipment items and is not used
to correct a nose-up or nose-down tendency of
an aircraft. It is usually located as far aft or as
far forward as possible in order to bring the
c.g. position within acceptable limits with a
minimum of weight increase. Permanent ballast is often lead plate wrapped around and
bolted to the fuselage primary structure (e.i.,
tail-post, longerons, or bulkhead members).
Permanent ballast invariably constitutes a concentrated load; therefore, the strength of the
local structure and the attachment of the ballast
thereto should be investigated for the design
loading conditions pertinent to that particular
aircraft. Placard permanent ballast with Permanent ballast - do not remove. It is not
