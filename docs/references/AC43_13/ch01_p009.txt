AC 43.13-1B  Chapter 1: WOOD STRUCTURE  Section 1: MATERIALS AND PRACTICES

1-9. BONDING TEMPERATURE. Temperature of the bond line affects the cure rate
of the adhesive. Some adhesive types, such as
resorcinol, require a minimum temperature
which must be maintained throughout the
curing process. Each type of adhesive requires
a specific temperature during the cure cycle,
and the manufacturer’s recommendations
should be followed.
