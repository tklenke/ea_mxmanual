AC 43.13-1B  Chapter 6: CORROSION, INSPECTION & PROTECTION  Section 2: TYPES OF CORROSION

6-18. EXFOLIATION CORROSION. Exfoliation corrosion is an advanced form of inter-granular corrosion and shows itself by lifting up the surface grains of a metal by the
force of expanding corrosion products occurring at the grain boundaries just below the surface. (See figure 6-10.) It is visible evidence
of inter-granular corrosion and is most often
seen on extruded sections where grain thickness are usually less than in rolled forms.
