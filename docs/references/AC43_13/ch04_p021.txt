4-21. INDENTATIONS. The Vickers indenter penetrates about twice as far into the
work piece as does the Knoop indenter. The
diagonal of the Vickers indentation is about
one-third of the total length of the Knoop indentation. The Vickers indenter is less sensitive to minute differences in surface conditions
than is the Knoop indenter. However, the
Vickers indentation, because of the shorter diagonal, is more sensitive to errors in measuring
than is the Knoop indentation. (See figure 4-1.)
