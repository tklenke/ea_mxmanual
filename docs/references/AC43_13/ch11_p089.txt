11-89. SHIELDED WIRE. With the increase in number of highly sensitive electronic
devices found on modern aircraft, it has become very important to ensure proper shielding for many electric circuits. Shielding is the
process of applying a metallic covering to
wiring and equipment to eliminate interference
caused by stray electromagnetic energy.
Shielded wire or cable is typically connected to
the aircraft’s ground at both ends of the wire,
or at connectors in the cable. Electromagnetic
Interference (EMI) is caused when electromagnetic fields (radio waves) induce highfrequency (HF) voltages in a wire or component. The induced voltage can cause system
inaccuracies or even failure, therefore putting
the aircraft and passengers at risk. Shielding
helps to eliminate EMI by protecting the primary conductor with an outer conductor. Refer to MIL-DTL-27500, Cable, Power, Electrical and Cable Special Purpose, Electrical
Shielded and Unshielded General Specifications.
